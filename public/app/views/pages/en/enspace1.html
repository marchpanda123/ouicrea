OuiCrea • CN-FR

Station F is the largest entrepreneurial space for start-ups in today's world. It was remodeled from the preserved structure of Halle Freyssinet, an old railway station in 1929 with a total area of 34,000 square meters. The project's investor is founder of the famous French telecommunications group Iliad-Free, France's largest investor Xavier Niel, a total investment of 250 million euros. After the completion of the station F within the French investment bank, the French post office, corporate laboratories, meeting rooms, classrooms, restaurants, bars, cafes, bathrooms and other space, can accommodate at least 1,000 emerging companies, 3,000 people.

Station F is located in the center of Paris, on the left bank of the Seine, next to the National Library of France and within 5 minutes of the metro, public electric bike / bike station, hospital, floating swimming pool, fitness center, cinema, art gallery, 5 pharmacies, 2 post offices and 2 supermarkets.

Station F venture space is divided into shared space, creative space and leisure space. The shared space includes an auditorium for up to 370 people, meeting rooms, telephone booths, sample preparation experiments (3D printing, laser cutting), coffee shops, venture capital agencies and several private start-ups. The creative space in the middle contains 3,000 work desks, 32 meeting rooms, 16 kitchen / lounges and 8 large incubators. The lounge has a restaurant, kitchen and bar, open 24 hours a day, offering seats for more than 1,000 people.

Station F's current partners are: Facebook, Amazon, VentePrivee, HEC, Hollande Foundation and other internationally renowned enterprises, universities, business organizations and so on. Eurocopter is Station F's only founding partner of China and France, joining Eurocopter, France and China into the top entrepreneurial ecosystem in France. In addition, Eurocopter launched the China-France Cross-border Incubation Program, which serves cross-border incubators between the two countries.
